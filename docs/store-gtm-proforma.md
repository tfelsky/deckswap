# Mythiverse Exchange Store GTM Plan and Pro Forma

Source: current `/pricing` page for Store Presence, Store Growth, Store Network, marketplace fees, add-ons, and launch offer.

## Quick GTM Plan

### Positioning

Mythiverse Exchange should launch as the operating layer for local game stores that want to turn Commander trade-ins, singles, events, screens, and local player demand into one connected revenue lane.

The clearest wedge is Store Growth: it is the recommended default, includes singles tools, one LGS TV screen license, PodMatch Level 1, two local email blasts, three staff logins, and local pickup/reservation flows. The first 20 partner stores can lock Growth at $199/month for 12 months, creating a concrete urgency mechanism.

### Ideal Customer Profile

- Local game stores with active Commander nights.
- Stores buying collections or trade-ins that create slow-moving singles/deck inventory.
- Stores already running weekly events but lacking polished digital scheduling, pod management, and local remarketing.
- Stores willing to test in-store screens or local pickup as a demand-generation loop.

### Initial Offer

Lead with a 30-day partner setup sprint:

- Store profile and event calendar live.
- First batch of singles or Commander deck inventory listed.
- One LGS TV screen rotation configured for Growth stores.
- First two opt-in local email blasts drafted and approved.
- PodMatch Level 1 event workflow used for one Commander night.

Commercial ask:

- Store Growth at the launch rate of $199/month for the first 20 stores.
- Annual conversion after the pilot with two months free.
- Network upsell for stores that need four screens, featured calendar placement, deeper reporting, and PodMatch Level 2.

### Channel Plan

1. Founder-led outbound to 100 qualified LGS targets in reachable MTG-heavy cities.
2. Demo around one visible store outcome: list inventory, publish an event, show it on LGS TV, and send a local blast.
3. Use first stores as city cluster anchors, then sell nearby stores on PodMatch and cross-store programming.
4. Co-market launch stores through featured calendar placement, store profiles, and player-facing event content.
5. Convert the first 20 Growth stores into case studies before pushing Network and PodMatch Level 3.

### 90-Day Execution

| Timing | Goal | Key actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weeks 1-2 | Prepare the sales motion | Finalize pricing one-pager, demo store, onboarding checklist, pilot email sequence, and target list of 100 LGS prospects. |
| Weeks 3-6 | Close first 5 stores | Sell Growth launch offer, onboard first inventory/events, collect activation metrics, and identify Network candidates. |
| Weeks 7-10 | Build first city cluster | Close 10-12 total stores, run PodMatch-backed Commander nights, and test local blasts against real attendance. |
| Weeks 11-13 | Fill first 20 launch slots | Convert launch customers, publish proof points, start annual-plan conversations, and package Network upsell. |

### Core Metrics

- Partner pipeline: qualified stores, demos booked, pilots started, paid conversions.
- Activation: days to first listing, first event published, first screen live, first blast sent.
- Store economics: monthly GMV per store, marketplace fee revenue, add-on attach rate, annual conversion rate.
- Retention: weekly active stores, event cadence, staff logins, repeat blast usage, churn.

## Readout

This plan intentionally makes Growth the acquisition product. Presence keeps the floor accessible, but the business works when stores adopt Growth and then graduate into Network, additional screens, PodMatch Level 3, and more local blasts.

The model reaches positive EBITDA in Year 3 under these assumptions. The key risks are store activation speed, whether marketplace GMV reaches the assumed per-store levels, and whether local email blasts and LGS TV screens attach at enough volume to become meaningful expansion revenue.
